Releasing Free Software if you work at a University

如果你在大学里面工作的话，发布自由软件吧

In the Free Software Movement, we believe computer users should have the freedom to change and redistribute the software that they use. The ``free'' in free software refers to freedom: it means users have the freedom to run, modify and redistribute the software. Free software contributes to human knowledge, while non-free software does not. Universities should therefore encourage free software for the sake of advancing human knowledge, just as they should encourage scientists and other scholars to publish their work.

自由软件运动中，我们相信电脑用户对所用软件，有修改和再发布的自由。“free software”中的“free”指的是自由，这意味着用户有运行、修改和再发布软件的自由。自由软件为人类知识做贡献，然而非自由软件则没有。为了促进人类知识的发展，大学应该理所当然的鼓励自由软件，比如鼓励科学家和其他学者公开分享他们的成果。

Alas, many university administrators have a grasping attitude towards software (and towards science); they see programs as opportunities for income, not as opportunities to contribute to human knowledge. Free software developers have been coping with this tendency for almost 20 years.

同样，许多大学的管理层人士对待软件和科学的态度是贪婪的，他们把软件看作是赚钱的机会，而不是为人类知识做贡献的机会。自由软件开发者们这种形式的拷贝共享，将近二十年了。

When I started developing the GNU operating system in 1984, my first step was to quit my job at MIT. I did this specifically so that the MIT licensing office would be unable to interfere with releasing GNU as free software. I had planned an approach for licensing the programs in GNU that ensures that all modified versions must be free software as well, an approach that developed into the GNU General Public License (GNU GPL), and I did not want to have to beg the MIT administration to let me use it.

当我1984年开始开发GNU 操作系统时，第一步就是辞掉我在MIT的工作。我这样做以后，MIT许可部门就不能干涉GNU系统以自由软件发布。我想出了一个方法，开发GNU 通用公共许可证，来确保在GNU系统发布的软件，其修改版本必须也是自由软件。而且我不用乞求MIT管理人士让我采用她。

Over the years, university affiliates have often come to the Free Software Foundation for advice on how to cope with administrators who see software only as something to sell. One good method, applicable even for specifically funded projects, is to base your work on an existing program that was released under the GNU GPL. Then you can tell the administrators, ``We're not allowed to release the modified version except under the GNU GPL -- any other way would be copyright infringement.'' After the dollar signs fade from their eyes, they will usually consent to releasing it as free software.

关于如何从坚持用软件赚钱的管理层人士那里拷贝分享软件，这些年来，经常有大学里的公司来自由软件基金会寻求建议。一个好的、可行的方法就是，基于以GNU GPL协议发布的程序来开发软件。这样你就可以告诉管理层人士，“我只能采用GNU GPL协议发布软件的修改版本。”当他们淡化金钱观念时，他们会同意把软件作为自由软件发布。

You can also ask your funding sponsor for help. When a group at NYU developed the GNU Ada Compiler, with funding from the U.S. Air Force, the contract explicitly called for donating the resulting code to the Free Software Foundation. Work out the arrangement with the sponsor first, then politely show the university administration that it is not open to renegotiation. They would rather have a contract to develop free software than no contract at all, so they will most likely go along.

你也可以向资金赞助商寻求帮助。在美国空防部赞助下，NYU有个小组在开发GNU Ada语言编译器，合约明确指明捐赠最终代码给自由软件基金会。首先和赞助商制订出安排，然后委婉的告诉大学管理层人士，违约是不行的。开发自由软件，他们更愿意签订一个合约，因此他们都会继续做下去。

Whatever you do, raise the issue early -- certainly before the program is half finished. At this point, the university still needs you, so you can play hardball: tell the administration you will finish the program, make it usable, if they have agreed in writing to make it free software (and agreed to your choice of free software license). Otherwise you will work on it only enough to write a paper about it, and never make a version good enough to release. When the administrators know their choice is to have a free software package that brings credit to the university or nothing at all, they will usually choose the former.

不管你做什么，早些提出问题，就是在程序完成一半之前。此时大学仍然需要你，因此你可以采取强硬措施：告诉管理层人士，如果他们同意以自由软件发布此软件（同意你选择自由软件许可证），你将完成此软件，使它变得有用。否则你将仅仅工作的足以写个关于它的论文，并且不会做出能够发行的版本。当管理层人士知道他们的选择会让自由软件包，携带对大学的信用或者什么也不是时，他们常常选择前者。

Not all universities have grasping policies. The University of Texas has a policy that makes it easy to release software developed there as free software under the GNU General Public License. Univates in Brazil, and the International Institute of Information Technology in Hyderabad, India, both have policies in favor of releasing software under the GPL. By developing faculty support first, you may be able to institute such a policy at your university. Present the issue as one of principle: does the university have a mission to advance human knowledge, or is its sole purpose to perpetuate itself?

不是所有大学都能领会方针政策。德克萨斯州大学有个政策，使得开发遵循GNU通用公共许可证的自由软件更加简单。巴西的大学、印度海得拉巴的国际信息技术学院，都有关于以GPL协议发布自由软件的政策。要动员大家支持，首先你得在你们大学研究这样一个政策。提出问题作为原则：大学是否有促进人类知识发展的使命，或者使其本身永存不朽的单一目标。

Whatever approach you use, it helps to have determination and adopt an ethical perspective, as we do in the Free Software Movement. To treat the public ethically, the software should be free -- as in freedom -- for the whole public.

不管你用什么方法，它使得做出决定，并像我们在自由软件运动中做的那样，从伦理道德的角度去看待问题。从公众道德角度考虑，软件对公众应该是自由的。

Many developers of free software profess narrowly practical reasons for doing so: they advocate allowing others to share and change software as an expedient for making software powerful and reliable. If those values motivate you to develop free software, well and good, and thank you for your contribution. But those values do not give you a good footing to stand firm when university administrators pressure or tempt you to make the program non-free.

许多自由软件开发者关于这种行为，宣称一种勉强地现实理由：他们主张用户把分享和修改软件作为使软件功能强大、可靠的临时手段。如果你以这种价值观开发自由软件，很好，谢谢你的贡献。但当大学管理人士压迫或者诱惑你把软件变得不自由时，这种价值观不会给你一个好的立足点。

For instance, they may argue that ``We could make it even more powerful and reliable with all the money we can get.'' This claim may or may not come true in the end, but it is hard to disprove in advance. They may suggest a license to offer copies ``free of charge, for academic use only,'' which would tell the general public they don't deserve freedom, and argue that this will obtain the cooperation of academia, which is all (they say) you need.

例如，他们可能同意用所有挣来的钱，促使软件功能强大、可靠。这种要求不知道能不能最终成为现实，但是很难证明在进步。他们可能会建议使用一种仅供学校使用、修改的许可证，这意为这公众不能得到自由，并争论这是否能带来一个经济合作，他们说这是你们所有人需要的。

If you start from ``pragmatic'' values, it is hard to give a good reason for rejecting these dead-end proposals, but you can do it easily if you base your stand on ethical and political values. What good is it to make a program powerful and reliable at the expense of users' freedom? Shouldn't freedom apply outside academia as well as within it? The answers are obvious if freedom and community are among your goals. Free software respects the users' freedom, while non-free software negates it.

如果你从太过现实的价值观出发，很难有个好的理由拒绝这些该死的协议，但如果你站在道德和政治的角度，这就很简单了。以用户自由为代价，换取软件的功能强大、可靠有什么好处？像在经济内部一样，自由能应用在经济以外吗？如果自由和社区是你的目标，那么答案就很明显。自由软件尊重用户的自由，然而非自由软件否认这一点。

Nothing strengthens your resolve like knowing that the community's freedom depends, in one instance, on you.

没有什么能来坚定你的决心，正如让你意识到某种情况下社区的自由是依靠你们的。
